Our Story

The story till now.

  • One Monday morning...

    Pedro first noticed Lucy as she arrived for a job interview at his place of work. Lucy on the other hand wondered why Pedro was staring at her (was it love at first sight Pedro?) She got the job and it wasn't long before he asked her to go for a drink with him. Lucy kept declining until one Friday she had nothing better to do and so their story began…

  • Meet the parents

    One bright, sunny summer's afternoon in 2009, Pedro met Lucy’s parents at The London Docklands while supporting Lucy who was taking part in a swimming event. It should be noted, Lucy did not inform Pedro he was going to meet her parents, and she did not inform her parents they were going to meet Pedro. Instead, she quickly introduced everyone before 'leaving them to it' while she swam.

    Lucy met Pedro’s Dad on her first trip to South Africa and was presented with a delicious Portuguese feast and plenty of wine – much more relaxing.

  • Cohabitation

    Lucy moved into Pedro’s shared house in north London while training to become a Speech and Language Therapist. At weekends, they’d brunch and blow the cobwebs away on their matching bikes, a habit they’re yet to grow out of.

    In June 2014, they moved to their first home in Blackheath, south east London. Excessive feathering the nest began until Pedro instilled a strict one in one out clutter policy.

  • Shiny

    Pedro finally proposed on 5th May 2017 during one of their many trips to South Africa. He walked Lucy into the clouds of Table Mountain before dropping to one knee. They skipped down the mountain ready for a celebratory red wine and steak.

Getting here

  • The closest airport is Cork Airport, 80km from Skibbereen. Cork Airport is a short flight from most UK airports. Ryanair and Aer-Lingus operate frequent direct flights. Please book your flights early to avoid high-cost fares. View some options on Google Flights.

  • We would recommend hiring a car, as transport options to Skibbereen are limited and travelling by car is the easiest way to see Skibbereen and it's surrounding areas. If you book early, car hire is very, very inexpensive. Cork Airport has a good list of car hire companies.

Things to do

Things to do in Skibbereen and the surrounding areas

Lucy has always loved Skibbereen, it’s where she got to spend six weeks every summer running around the countryside and swimming in the sea. Pedro and Lucy now visit Ireland once or twice a year as a welcomed escape from London. Here are some suggestions of their favourite things to do. Definitely bring your raincoat if you are planning on doing anything other than staying in the town. It rains a lot in West Cork but don’t worry, it will toughen you up.

Pubs

Skibbereen has plenty of really really great pubs, here are a couple of suggestions:

  • Tanyard, Skibbereen. A busy pub with live music most evenings.
  • The Corner Bar, Skibbereen. Traditional Irish pub with live music a few nights a week.
  • Cahalane’s Bar, Skibbereen.

Restaurants

  • The Church, Skibbereen. A converted church with pretty stained glass windows and tasty food in the centre of the Skibbereen. Book before you go as it’s popular.
  • An Cistin Beag, Skibbereen. A lovely informal reasonably priced place to eat serving tasty breakfast, lunch or dinner.
  • Annie Mays, Skibbereen. Delicious pub grub – you definitely won’t go hungry.
  • Glandore Inn, Glandore. Approximately 13km from Skibbereen a lovely little restaurant.
    • overlooking the harbour. Get there while it’s still bright to soak up the view. It’s a small restaurant so bookings are recommended.
  • Bistro, Glandore. Highly recommended.

Skibbereen Heritage Centre

  • Skibbereen Heritage centre in the town is well worth a visit. It’s a great place to find information on Loch Hyne, one of Lucy’s favourite swimming spots, a unique, saltwater, tidal marine lake.
  • The Centre also offers a sobering account of The Great Famine, a national tragedy that had a devastating impact on Skibbereen during the 19th century.

Food Shopping

  • Fields is the main supermarket in the town centre. They have a lovely bakery as well as fresh fish, meat and cheese counters.
  • If you’re looking for fresh, fresh fish, The Fish Station, Marsh Road, Skibbereen is great.
  • Lidl is also on the one-way system.

Lough Hyne

  • A short 5km drive southwest of Skibbereen, Loch Hyne is a beautiful and peaceful tidal marine lake. It was designated as Europe’s first marine nature reserve and is up to 52 metres deep.
  • Look out for the tiny car park/layby looking area on the right hand side as you approach the Loch. Park here for the start of a 40 minute uphill walk, through pine trees, to a 360 degree view of Loch Hyne and the surrounding areas.

Glandore and Union Hall

  • Small pretty fishing villages steeped in history approximately 10km from Skibbereen.
  • Drombeg Stone Circle. 17 standing stones dating back to around 150 BC, a popular spot for Winter Solstice when at sunset the sun’s rays fall directly on the altar stone.

Sheeps Head

  • A famous peninsula with beautiful scenery, head out towards Bantry and follow the signs. There are lots of long and short walking routes available around Sheeps Head.

The Coast

  • It’s well worth just heading out towards the coast and driving around the beautiful headlands. Bring a map, get a bit lost and just enjoy it. Lucy’s favourite place is Toe Head, it overlooks the famous Fasnet Lighthouse, see if you can find it.

Gouganebarra

  • A longer 53km drive from Skibbereen, Gouganebarra is a serene lake surrounded by hills with a tiny, picturesque church and ample walking trails. It is definitely worth venturing out to if you have the time, its just beautiful.

Bantry

  • 30km from Skibbereen, Bantry is the home town of Graham Norton, it has pretty views but is also a bit of a drive.

Kinsale

  • Approximately 66km from Skibbereen and 20km from Cork Airport, Kinsale is a yachting harbour and is gastronomically renowned… don’t forget your boat shoes!
